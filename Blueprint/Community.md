# TWE Blueprint – Community

## Purpose

A Community is the fundamental building block of Troglodyte Works.

Everything within TWE exists to help a community become more welcoming, organized, sustainable, and enjoyable for its members.

Whether the community is two friends playing Minecraft, a high school robotics team, a classroom, a family, a gaming guild, or a nonprofit organization, the experience should feel familiar.

---

# Philosophy

Communities are built by people.

Technology should remove friction, preserve knowledge, and make it easier for people to spend time together.

TWE exists to equip community leaders—not replace them.

---

# Every Community Contains

## Home

The current state of the community.

This answers:

- What is happening now?
- What needs attention?
- What has changed recently?

The Home page is intentionally simple and should never overwhelm the user.

---

## Meetings

A chronological history of discussions.

Meetings may include:

- Notes
- AI summaries
- Action items
- Recordings
- Attendance
- Linked decisions

The Meetings section becomes the living memory of conversations.

---

## Decisions

Every important decision deserves a permanent home.

Each decision should answer:

- What was decided?
- Why was it decided?
- When?
- By whom?
- Has anything changed since?

Communities often forget why something exists.

TWE should never forget.

---

## Playbooks

Playbooks capture repeatable knowledge.

Examples:

- How to onboard a new member.
- How to restart an ARK server.
- How to prepare for a robotics competition.
- How to organize a community event.

A Playbook represents experience becoming reusable knowledge.

---

## Projects

Projects represent active work.

Each project contains:

- Goals
- Milestones
- Tasks
- Owners
- Progress

Projects eventually finish.

Their lessons become Decisions and Playbooks.

---

## Resources

Resources store information that supports the community.

Examples:

- Documents
- Images
- Videos
- External links
- Reference material
- Templates

Resources support the work but are not the work itself.

---

# The Role of Trog

Trog is not the community.

Trog is the guide.

Trog observes activity and quietly suggests organization.

Examples:

> "Would you like me to save that as a Decision?"

> "This sounds like a new Project."

> "I've noticed this process repeats often. Would you like me to create a Playbook?"

Trog helps people stay organized without demanding structure.

---

# Design Principles

- Simplicity over complexity.
- Organize only when helpful.
- Never overwhelm new users.
- Allow communities to grow naturally.
- Preserve history.
- Make knowledge easy to rediscover.
- AI should assist, not control.

---

# Future Vision

As a community matures, TWE becomes its institutional memory.

New members can quickly understand:

- where the community has been,
- how it operates,
- what it values,
- and where it is going.

The goal is not simply better organization.

The goal is helping communities become places where people feel welcome, connected, and eager to return.
