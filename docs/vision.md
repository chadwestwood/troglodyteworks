# Troglodyte Works Vision

Troglodyte Works helps gaming communities coordinate, automate, and manage their shared worlds.

It is not only a game server hosting platform.

Hosting is one capability within a larger system designed to support community owners, server admins, moderators, players, creators, and gaming groups.

---

## North Star

Empower community leaders and members to confidently manage their gaming communities without needing to become systems administrators.

---

## Core Philosophy

Troglodyte Works should feel:

- Calm
- Simple
- Powerful

Power should be discovered, not endured.

The product should adapt to where members are starting instead of forcing members to learn the platform before receiving value.

---

## What Troglodyte Works Supports

Troglodyte Works may support:

- Hosted game servers
- Existing external servers
- Discord-connected communities
- Community events
- Polls and decisions
- Server automation
- Server settings
- Mods and plugins
- Backups
- Monitoring
- Member roles
- Guided assistance

---

## First Supported Games

- ARK Survival Ascended
- ARK Survival Evolved
- Palworld
- Minecraft

Future games may be added as services without changing the core mission.

---

## Visitors and Members

A Visitor has no account and is exploring.

A Member has an identity and may belong to one or more communities.

Members may have different roles in different communities, such as:

- Community Member
- Moderator
- Community Owner
- Server Owner
- Administrator

Billing and subscriptions are separate from identity.

Someone can receive value before becoming a paying customer.

---

## Guidance Philosophy

Trog is the default Guide.

Guides help members accomplish goals without making them feel incapable.

A Guide is not a teacher standing over the user.

A Guide is a hand reaching out when needed.

The product must remain excellent even if Guides are disabled.

Guides should adapt over time based on how a member prefers to be helped.

---

## Product Direction

Troglodyte Works should be organized around user journeys, not just pages.

Key journeys include:

- Join Friends
- Manage Game Servers
- Explore Troglodyte Works

Each journey should ask one clear question at a time and reveal only the next relevant step.

The interface should never require users to learn Troglodyte Works vocabulary before they can receive value.

---

## Long-Term Goal

Troglodyte Works should become the operating layer for gaming communities:

- Web portal when visual control is useful
- Discord integration when community interaction is natural
- MCP tools when automation is needed
- Guides when help is useful

The goal is not to overwhelm users with control panels.

The goal is to help communities feel organized, confident, and capable.
