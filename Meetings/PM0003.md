# Purpose

Explore the onboarding experience and define the core philosophy of Troglodyte Works.

---

# Major Discoveries

## 1. Troglodyte Works is not primarily a hosting platform.

The discussion concluded that hosting is one capability of a much larger product.

The larger mission is to help gaming communities coordinate, automate, and manage their activities regardless of where their servers are hosted.

Hosting, Discord integration, AI guidance, automation, and server management are all services supporting that mission.

---

## 2. The product should be organized around User Journeys instead of pages.

Rather than designing screens first, Troglodyte Works should guide members through journeys based on what they are trying to accomplish.

Examples include:

- Join friends
- Manage game servers
- Explore Troglodyte Works

Each journey branches naturally into more specific questions.

---

## 3. Onboarding should feel like a conversation.

The landing page should avoid large menus and long scrolling pages.

Instead it should present a single question followed by only the next relevant question.

The previous question disappears as the next one appears.

This reinforces the design philosophy:

Calm.
Simple.
Powerful.

---

## 4. First Question Philosophy

Instead of asking users to understand Troglodyte Works, ask about their goal.

Initial concepts include:

- Join Friends
- Game Servers
- Explore

Each choice begins a different journey.

---

## 5. Visitors and Members

Identity should remain simple.

Visitor

- No identity
- Browsing
- Exploring

Member

- Authenticated
- May belong to one or more communities
- May own servers
- May manage communities

Roles are separate from identity.

Examples:

- Community Member
- Moderator
- Community Owner
- Server Owner
- Administrator

---

## 6. Communities

Communities own invitations.

A Discord invitation should naturally lead into Troglodyte Works authentication and automatically place the new Member into the appropriate community.

---

## 7. Guidance Philosophy

The product should always function without requiring a Guide.

Guides are companions rather than instructors.

They assist when needed but never become mandatory.

---

## 8. Guide Evolution

Trog is the default Guide.

Members may manually change Guides if desired.

However, Guides should naturally evolve by observing interaction patterns.

Examples:

- Trog notices repeated requests for deeper explanations and recommends Athena.

- Trog notices the Member consistently skips explanations and recommends Forge.

The Guide relationship evolves naturally rather than requiring configuration.

---

## 9. Teaching Philosophy

The project intentionally avoids making Members feel like students.

Guides should never imply incompetence.

Instead they provide assistance appropriate to the Member's preferred interaction style.

The software adapts to how Members prefer to be helped.

---

## 10. Product Philosophy

Software should not force Members to learn the product.

The product should adapt itself to Members.

# Decisions

✔ Use "Guide" instead of "Teacher"

✔ Keep Trog as the default Guide

✔ Allow Guides to evolve naturally

✔ Organize onboarding around journeys

✔ Build confidence rather than teach software

✔ The product should work perfectly even if Guides are disabled

✔ Focus on user goals rather than product features
