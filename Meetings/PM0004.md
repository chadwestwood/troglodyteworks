# Meeting Purpose

Continue transitioning Troglodyte Works from product discovery into implementation by refining the user experience philosophy before additional development.

The discussion focused on how members should experience Troglodyte Works rather than how the software should function internally.

---

# Decisions Made

## 1. Multiple Entry Points

The team recognized that the landing page is only one possible entry into Troglodyte Works.

Three primary entry points were identified.

### Discovery

Visitors arriving from:

- Search engines
- Advertisements
- Recommendations

These visitors begin with the conversational onboarding experience.

---

### Invitation

Visitors arriving through:

- Discord invitations
- Steam invitations
- Invite codes
- Shared community links

These visitors should bypass the marketing-style onboarding.

Their experience becomes:

Invitation

↓

Authentication

↓

Community

The destination is the Community—not Troglodyte Works itself.

---

### Returning Members

Returning Members should authenticate and be returned directly to their most recent Community or workspace.

No onboarding should be repeated.

---

# 2. Community Is The Destination

One of the most significant philosophical decisions made during this meeting was recognizing that Members are not attempting to arrive at Troglodyte Works.

Members are attempting to arrive at:

- their friends
- their Community
- their server
- their shared world

Troglodyte Works should become almost invisible during this transition.

---

# 3. Community Switcher

The previous concept of a simple community dropdown evolved into a much more important navigation component.

The Community Switcher should:

- Display the Member's current Community.
- Allow switching between Communities.
- Remember the Member's current context.
- Become the primary navigation context for the application.

The Community Switcher represents "where I am" rather than "what I want to select."

---

# 4. Awareness Instead of Notifications

Traditional notification systems encourage interruption.

Troglodyte Works should instead provide awareness.

Examples include:

- Recent community activity
- Upcoming events
- Poll results
- Server status

Members should receive information when it helps them decide what to do next—not simply because new information exists.

---

# 5. Respect Attention

A new design philosophy emerged.

Member attention should be treated as a limited and valuable resource.

Troglodyte Works should avoid:

- intrusive notifications
- unnecessary popups
- unread counters as primary navigation
- interrupting Members from their current task

Information should appear only when it becomes useful.

---

# 6. Awareness Navigator

The Community Switcher should eventually evolve beyond navigation.

When a Member considers switching Communities, the interface may provide lightweight awareness such as:

Bob's Minecraft
• Community vote completed

Family Server
• Quiet

Friday Night Gamers
• Event tonight

This provides context without demanding attention.

---

# 7. Product Voice

The team discussed separating visual design from conversational design.

The language used throughout Troglodyte Works should feel:

- calm
- confident
- helpful
- conversational

The interface itself should behave like a Guide, even when no Guide character is visible.

---

# Example Language

Preferred:

Every community starts somewhere.

Let's find yours.

---

Avoid:

FIRST QUESTION

NEXT QUESTION

Dashboard

Unread Notifications

The language should sound like a trusted guide rather than a software company.

---

# 8. Guides

An important realization occurred during implementation.

The personality of Troglodyte Works should not depend on Trog being visible.

Instead:

The interface itself becomes the Guide.

Guide characters simply personalize that experience.

This allows the platform to remain useful regardless of which Guide a Member chooses.

---

# Key Insight

The discussion produced a significant product philosophy:

People do not care about every activity.

They care when an activity becomes relevant to the decision they are making.

Troglodyte Works should therefore optimize for attention rather than engagement.

# Meeting Outcome

This meeting marked an important transition in the design of Troglodyte Works.

The platform shifted from thinking about pages and features toward thinking about attention, context, and human decision-making.

Future design work should continue asking:

"Where is this Member right now, and what is the next useful thing they need?"
